Modernizing the Powder Transfer System

This project outlines the conversion of the Powder Transfer System's (PCS) lower control panel from a traditional, hard-wired relay system to a modern, flexible, and highly protected embedded system. The goal is to enhance reliability, simplify maintenance, and build a foundation for future automation and data integration.

Key Benefits

  • Enhanced flexibility via software-based logic
  • Improved user experience with a central HMI
  • Reduced wiring complexity and maintenance
  • Foundation for Industry 4.0 and IoT integration
  • Increased system reliability and safety

Core Technology Shift

The project replaces discrete timers, relays, and indicators with a centralized Selec Flexys GT35 Modular PLC with an integrated Graphical Display and Touch Screen. This consolidates control into a single, programmable unit, leveraging the existing robust 24VDC power supply.

Modern Embedded PLC System

Integrated HMI Display

A single 3.5" touch screen replaces all individual indicator lamps, providing a dynamic, centralized view of system status, alarms, and real-time data.

HMI-Based Timers & Setpoints

All timing parameters for charging, discharging, and purging are configured via the intuitive touch screen interface, replacing physical digital timers.

Software-Defined PLC Logic

Complex hard-wired relay logic is converted into flexible, easily modifiable software (Ladder Logic) running on the PLC, drastically reducing wiring and simplifying maintenance.

Modular Digital I/O

Push buttons and other operator controls are wired to the PLC's digital input modules. Their functions are managed by the software, allowing for greater control flexibility.

Hard-Wired Safety Circuit

Crucially, the Emergency Stop remains a hard-wired circuit. It bypasses the PLC software to directly cut power via a safety relay, ensuring maximum, fail-safe protection independent of software state.

Highly Protected Circuit Design

The new embedded system is designed with multiple layers of protection to ensure reliability and safety in a harsh industrial environment. Hover over each layer to learn more.

Galvanic Isolation

Opto-couplers on all I/O lines create an electrical barrier, protecting the sensitive PLC from voltage spikes and noise from field devices like motors and relays.

EMI/RFI Filtering

Power line filters and shielded cables are used to suppress high-frequency electrical noise, preventing interference with digital signals and ensuring stable operation.

Overvoltage & Overcurrent Protection

TVS Diodes clamp transient voltage spikes, while individual fuses on each output line protect against short circuits and overloads, isolating faults to a single circuit.

Environmental & Mechanical Protection

The system is housed in a sealed SS304 enclosure (IP66 rated) with vibration damping to protect against dust, moisture, and mechanical stress.

Implementation Roadmap

A structured, four-phase approach ensures a smooth and successful transition from the legacy system to the modernized embedded control panel.

1

Design & Prototyping

Detailed electrical design, PLC/HMI software development, and rigorous testing of a prototype system in a lab environment.

2

Fabrication & Assembly

Fabrication of the new panel and meticulous assembly and wiring of the PLC, HMI, and all associated components.

3

Installation & Commissioning

On-site installation of the new panel and comprehensive commissioning tests to ensure correct operation in the field.

4

Training & Handover

Thorough training for operators and maintenance staff on the new system, followed by the handover of all documentation.
